SOCIAL ENGINEERING Estimated duration: 3 mins

Case 01: The Urgent Bank OTP Trap

Rahul receives an automated call claiming to be his local banking branch. They alert him to a "suspicious charge of ₹45,000" originating outside his location. To freeze the transaction, the voice tells him to share the 6-digit One-Time Passcode (OTP) sent to his mobile.

❌

Incorrect & Dangerous

By sharing the OTP, Rahul gave the scammer full access to approve a fraudulent transaction. Banks will never ask for your OTP, PIN, or CVV over the phone. This urgency tactic is designed to induce panic.

✅

Correct Decision

Rahul stayed calm and avoided acting on urgency—a classic social engineering tactic. By calling the official number on his bank card, he verified his account was safe without compromising his details.

ACADEMIC PHISHING Estimated duration: 2 mins

Case 02: The Spoofed College Admin Portal

Priya receives an email from "accounts-verif-avanzocollege.in" with the headline: "Warning: Unpaid Semester Fees—Profile Deactivation Imminent." It contains a portal login button to verify the fee slip.

❌

Incorrect & Dangerous

The link directed Priya to a fake website designed to harvest her login credentials. Scammers often spoof domains (like using "-avanzocollege" instead of the official domain) and use urgency to bypass critical thinking.

✅

Correct Decision

Priya correctly identified the suspicious sender address. By manually navigating to the official portal instead of trusting the email link, she bypassed the phishing trap and confirmed her account status safely.
